Natural Language Processing (NLP) encapsulates many techniques for computers to deal with human language

They include Sentiment Analysis, Stopword Removal, and Named Entity Recogniton

Sentiment Analysis:

Detecting whether text conveys a positive, negative, or neutral feeling. It's hard for computers to understand the connotations behind words

Stopword Removal:

Removing common words (like "the", "and", "is") that aren't important to the meaning of a sentence. This reduces noise, making the processing of text more efficient

Named Entity Recognition:

Identifying and classifying real-world objects in text (like names, places, and dates). For a computer, text is a sequence of many strings, so this helps structure it into useful information
